  • October 11, 2018

Genesis is by far the most popular WordPress theme framework. It offers several Genesis child themes for blogs, businesses, and magazines. They are available for education, real estate, fashion, church websites, and others but blogs are where the Genesis Framework shines the brightest.

There are no clear statistics on how many WordPress websites are powered by Genesis, but most bloggers I follow are using it. We often hear the expression “so simple that a child can use it.” This is not a cliché, and it should be the principal slogan of any programmer. It is not a secret that technology continues to evolve at a staggering rate. Every five years or so, a new great leap forward in technology occurs, and it shows no sign of slowing down.

Due to this exponential rise in complexity, it can be hard for people to keep up. In a poetic turn of fate, we now need more technology to operate the technology that we already have. These days, getting a hold of what’s online, preferably what’s trending, is a way to survive. Everyone must create an online profile that best represents their service or product.

Here is where WordPress themes can offer you a great advantage. Most themes that are available today are not only extremely well coded, but they are also very easy to use. Their frameworks make it easy to integrate social media platforms. The customization process is akin to going over a checklist. That being said, let’s take a look at some of the best Genesis Framework child themes.

Please note: You need to have Genesis Framework installed before you can start using any of the listed themes below. These themes are not going to work as standalone themes, so you need to have access to the framework itself.
